Demographics of Western Australia between 2011 and 2017

Western Australia is the largest state by land area, and the 4th largest state by populationin Australia.

Over the period of the early 2010s, Western Australia saw a large population growth as a result of the mining boom, which ended in 2014. The boom brought about large population growth, and hence house prices across WA have dramatically increased.

In 2017-18, two of the three local government areas with the highest median total income in Australia were in Western Australia. These LGAs were, Ashburton and Peppermint Grove, and Roxby Downs in South Australia.

Greater Perth increased by 37,600 people (1.8%), the rest of the state increased by 2,700 (0.5%).

Western Australia has one of the highest employment rates of the states in Australia.

In 2016, 65.90% of the total population were engaged in full time or part time work, with an unemployment rate of 5.5%. The employment rate uses the total population that includes seniors, students and children. The employment rate of Australia is 62.20%.

Unemployment rose in Western Australia between 2011 and 2016, with Regional WA experiencing an increase of 1.1% in unemployment over the five-year period.

Areas with the highest unemployment rates are the Kimberley and the Gascoyne Regions, and lowest in the Pilbara.
